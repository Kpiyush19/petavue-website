ROAS Skill
Agent analysis of 7-day ROAS, budget utilization, and ARR close rates by campaign
ROAS · 01
Pause campaigns breaching the 1.8× ROAS floor immediately
Key Insight
7-day ROAS < 1.8× on any active campaign
▲Margin risk: Negative contribution margin on every dollar spent below 1.8×
Any campaign sitting below a 1.8× 7-day ROAS is spending into negative contribution margin. Pause it today, redirect that daily budget to your highest-ROAS ad set, and document the pause for the pipeline review so the team is not treating stale spend as active pipeline fuel.
Recommendation
Identify all campaigns below the 1.8× threshold. Pause each one, log the budget freed, and reallocate to the top-performing ad set before end of day.
ROAS · 02
Your highest-ROAS B2B campaign is under-delivering — increase its budget before the week's pipeline window closes
Key Insight
Top B2B campaign ROAS ≥ 3.5× with < 80% of weekly budget consumed by Wednesday
▲Pipeline opportunity cost: A constrained 3.5× ROAS campaign targeting ICP accounts creates more qualified pipeline per dollar than any other active campaign
When a campaign reaching your target job titles and company sizes is hitting 3.5× ROAS but only consuming 75% of its weekly budget, it has room to reach more in-market accounts before the week ends. Increasing the daily cap by 25% on a constrained top performer recovers pipeline that would otherwise be missed entirely.
Recommendation
Identify any ICP-targeted campaign above 3.5× ROAS with budget utilization below 80% at midweek. Increase the daily cap by 25% and monitor delivery through Thursday.
ROAS · 03
Recalculate ROAS against closed ARR — not just pipeline value
Key Insight
Lead-to-closed-won: Google 14%, Meta 4%, LinkedIn 11%. Avg ARR/deal: $34K
▲ARR leakage: Campaigns with sub-5% close rates are generating false ROAS — real ARR contribution is 3× lower than reported
Meta campaigns are closing at a 4% lead-to-won rate vs. Google Search at 14%. At an avg ARR of $34K per deal, every 10 Meta leads that fail to close represents $136K in pipeline that never becomes revenue. ROAS needs to be measured at closed ARR before this week's budget decisions are made.
Recommendation
Pull lead-to-closed-won rate by campaign from CRM for the last 90 days. Recalculate ROAS using closed ARR. Flag any campaign where pipeline ROAS exceeds ARR ROAS by more than 1.5×.
ROAS · 04
Flag campaigns where SQLs stall in the pipeline with no sales activity within 14 days
Key Insight
Campaigns with > 40% SQL stall rate at "Opportunity Created" stage despite ROAS above floor
▲Sales activity gap: High ROAS campaigns may be feeding a dead end if SQLs show no activity within 14 days of creation
A campaign can look efficient on ROAS while quietly generating leads that stall at the opportunity stage. If 40%+ of a campaign's SQLs sit untouched for 14 days, the media efficiency number is misleading. The real conversion rate to closed-won may be near zero.
Recommendation
Pull opportunity stage distribution and last activity date by campaign source from CRM. Flag any campaign where > 40% of SQLs show no sales activity within 14 days. Bring the list to the pipeline meeting as a campaign quality audit.
